Animation · Ongoing Creative Practice

Animation Studies

Animation is where illustration, storytelling, pacing, and feeling meet. It taught me to think in sequence: how a scene starts, what viewers notice first, how timing changes emotion, and how small movements carry personality.

01 / Why animation

Learning to think across time, not just one frame.

Animation is where illustration, storytelling, pacing, and feeling meet for me. It asks different questions than a still image: what enters first, what lingers, where emotion shifts, and how sound or silence changes the mood of a sequence.

02 / What it changed

Sequence became part of my design thinking.

Working in motion taught me to plan cause and effect more deliberately. I began paying closer attention to rhythm, transitions, and how viewers move through a story beat by beat instead of all at once.

Storyboard

Find the emotional path before polishing.

Storyboards help me shape what matters first: the sequence of attention, the pacing of a scene, and how visual ideas connect from one moment to the next. This is where abstract ideas start becoming workable structure.

Thinking through composition, rhythm, and scene order before moving into animation.

Design prep

Character sheets become the logic behind motion.

Before movement feels convincing, the character has to feel consistent. Building character sheets helped me think about proportions, expression range, and how a drawn personality holds together across multiple moments.

Useful for key poses, continuity, and making later motion choices feel intentional.

Motion tests

Test timing, revise, then test again.

Motion studies were where I learned the most. Small tests helped me understand how timing changes emotion, how key moments need breathing room, and how even a slight pause or expression shift can change the whole feeling.

Built through After Effects and Clip Studio Paint by making, adjusting, and revising in public view.

Final piece

The Summer turned process into something shareable.

The Summer brought storyboarding, pacing, sound, and emotion together into a finished piece. The final work was selected for the Wake the Screen Student Exhibition, which made a childhood dream feel unexpectedly real.

A project that let me turn an inner idea into a sequence other people could enter and feel.
05 / What this shows about me

What animation adds to the way I work.

  • I can think visually across time, not just static layouts.
  • I care about pacing, emotion, and how viewers experience a sequence.
  • I'm comfortable learning tools through making, testing, and revising.
  • I bring illustration, design, and storytelling together.
