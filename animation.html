Animation · Artist story

The Summer

An original animation exploring visual storytelling through movement, pacing, sound, and emotion. The final film was selected for the Wake the Screen Student Exhibition.

01 / Artist statement

Making an imagined world move.

I use animation to translate feelings and abstract ideas into sequences an audience can follow. I am especially interested in how pacing, sound, gesture, and small changes between frames can carry emotion.

02 / Personal connection

A childhood interest, taken seriously.

I have loved animation since I was young. DreamWorks' Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron was an early inspiration: its movement, landscapes, and emotional storytelling stayed with me and also deepened my passion for horse riding. Creating The Summer was a way to honor that long-held dream.

03 / Process

From a feeling to a workflow.

I broke an abstract idea into workable stages: concept development, character studies, storyboard beats, timing, keyframes, sound, revision, and final editing. Working across After Effects and Clip Studio Paint helped me practice animation fundamentals while building a repeatable creative process.

04 / Outcome

Wake the Screen

Selected for the student exhibition.

Having the film shown publicly fulfilled a childhood dream. More importantly, it taught me how persistence and structure can turn a personal idea into a finished experience that connects with other people.
