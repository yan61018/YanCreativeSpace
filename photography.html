Photography · Ongoing Creative Practice

Visual Notes Across Places

Photography is a way for me to slow down and notice how culture appears in small details: signs, colors, gestures, light, storefronts, streets, and everyday rhythm.

01 / How it started

A camera from my dad changed the way I look.

I inherited my first Canon camera from my dad and began shooting Threads of Yunnan: Life in Motion in 2023. That was when I fell in love with street photography and with finding sparkle in mundane, everyday life.

02 / What I look for

I photograph the texture of ordinary life.

Photography helps me slow down enough to notice tone, rhythm, and cultural detail. A storefront, a reflected color, the way people cross a street, or the feeling of a block at a certain hour can say a lot about place without needing a big explanation.

03 / Visual thinking notes

The details I keep returning to.

  • color relationships
  • light and shadow
  • ordinary gestures
  • signs and storefronts
  • people moving through space
  • small cultural details

04 / What this shows about me

What photography adds to the way I work.

  • I notice details that other people might pass by.
  • I use visual observation as a form of audience and cultural research.
  • I care about tone, place, and the emotional texture of everyday life.
  • I bring a photographer's eye into marketing, design, and storytelling.
