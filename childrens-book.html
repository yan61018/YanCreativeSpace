Children's book project / FYS 111

I Don't Really Like Myself

A children's book exploring self-image, representation, and the emotional power of early stories.

Read the full book ↗

01 / Context

Books can be mirrors and windows.

I created this complete children's book for FYS 111, where we studied early education and the role stories play in helping children understand themselves and others.

When children see themselves represented, they can feel more seen and valued.

02 / Why it matters

Early stories stay with us.

Growing up with animated children's stories showed me how deeply those first characters and feelings can remain with us. This project asks how illustration can meet self-doubt with humor, recognition, and care.

That interest later connected naturally to my work with the Skip Prosser READ Program.

03 / Final book

A small story of noticing, reframing, and self-acceptance.

04 / What this shows

Communication can be an act of care.

  • I care about how visual storytelling shapes identity and belonging.
  • I connect concept, audience, and visual tone into one cohesive project.
  • I think about communication not only as design, but as care.
